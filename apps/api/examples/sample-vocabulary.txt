Sample Vocabulary List — Daily English for Beginners
======================================================
Use this file as a reference for how to structure a vocabulary import.
Drop a PDF or Word (.docx) version of this format into Admin → Import Document.

FORMAT: Each word or phrase on its own line, followed by its definition.
Blank lines between entries are fine. The AI parser extracts the word–definition
pairs and auto-generates multiple-choice and match-style questions.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GREETINGS & INTRODUCTIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Hello
A common English greeting used when meeting someone.

Good morning
A greeting used in the morning, before noon.

Good afternoon
A greeting used after noon and before evening.

Good evening
A greeting used in the evening or at night.

Goodbye
What you say when leaving someone or ending a conversation.

How are you?
A polite question asking about someone's wellbeing.

Nice to meet you
A phrase said when you are introduced to someone for the first time.

My name is ___
The phrase used to introduce yourself by name.

What is your name?
A question used to ask for someone's name.

See you later
An informal phrase meaning "we will meet again soon."

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
NUMBERS & TIME
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

One, Two, Three
The first three cardinal numbers in English.

Morning
The early part of the day, from sunrise to noon.

Afternoon
The part of the day from noon to evening.

Evening
The part of the day from late afternoon to night.

Today
The current day; this present day.

Tomorrow
The day after today.

Yesterday
The day before today.

Week
A period of seven days.

Month
A period of approximately 30 days; one of the 12 divisions of a year.

Year
A period of 365 days (or 366 in a leap year).

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOOD & DRINK
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Breakfast
The first meal of the day, eaten in the morning.

Lunch
The meal eaten in the middle of the day.

Dinner
The main meal of the day, usually eaten in the evening.

Water
A clear, tasteless liquid that is essential for life.

Coffee
A hot drink made from roasted coffee beans.

Tea
A hot drink made by pouring boiling water over dried tea leaves.

Rice
A common grain food eaten as a staple in many countries.

Bread
A food made from flour, water, and yeast, baked in an oven.

Fruit
The sweet or sour edible part of a plant (e.g., apple, mango, banana).

Vegetables
Edible plants or parts of plants, such as carrots, tomatoes, and spinach.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PLACES & DIRECTIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

School
A place where students go to learn.

Hospital
A building where doctors and nurses care for sick or injured people.

Market
A place where people buy and sell goods.

Bank
A place where people keep money and carry out financial transactions.

Left
The direction opposite to right; the side of the heart.

Right
The direction opposite to left.

Straight
Going directly ahead without turning.

Near
Close in distance; not far away.

Far
A long distance away; not near.

Turn
To change direction, usually at a corner or junction.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
COMMON VERBS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Eat
To put food in the mouth, chew, and swallow it.

Drink
To take liquid into the mouth and swallow it.

Go
To move or travel from one place to another.

Come
To move toward a person or place.

See
To perceive something with the eyes.

Hear
To perceive sound with the ears.

Speak
To use the voice to say words; to talk.

Listen
To give attention to a sound; to make an effort to hear.

Read
To look at and understand written words or symbols.

Write
To form letters or words on a surface with a pen, pencil, or keyboard.
